 Case Study: Phishing


Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing scenario begins with the victim receiving an email or message that appears to be from a legitimate organization, such as a bank or a popular company like Paytm. The message usually contains an urgent warning, such as “Your account will be blocked,” and includes a link. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. The victim then enters their login details, which are captured by the attacker. In some cases, the attacker may also request an OTP to complete unauthorized transactions.

Phishing attacks typically target students, elderly people, and individuals who are less aware of digital security practices. These groups are more likely to trust such messages and act quickly without verification.

The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may also face emotional stress and loss of trust in digital platforms.

This case highlights the importance of verifying links, avoiding suspicious messages, and never sharing sensitive information online.
